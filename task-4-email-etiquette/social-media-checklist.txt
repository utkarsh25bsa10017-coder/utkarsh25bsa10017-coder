Responsible Social Media Use: The College Student Checklist
The "Do's" (Best Practices)
•	Do audit your privacy settings regularly to control who can see your personal posts and tags.
•	Do assume everything you post is permanent, even if it is on a temporary story or a private account.
•	Do build a positive, professional presence on platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects and skills.
•	Do engage respectfully in online discussions, keeping your tone civil even when you disagree.
•	Do verify information, news, and links before sharing them to avoid spreading misinformation or scams.
The "Don'ts" (Things to Avoid)
•	Don't post or share anything involving illegal activities, underage drinking, or compromising situations.
•	Don't complain publicly about your professors, university, classmates, or internship employers.
•	Don't share sensitive personal information like your home address, phone number, or daily class schedule.
•	Don't engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or heated public arguments that leave a negative digital footprint.
•	Don't rely on a single social media feed as your only source of news without checking reputable sources
