
For this task, I used Google Forms to create a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz designed to evaluate my batchmates' understanding of essential online practices. Google Forms was the ideal tool because it allows for easy distribution and automated grading.

The quiz covers fundamental aspects of digital literacy, including cybersecurity, professional communication, and online identity. I included three multiple-choice questions focusing on the importance of two-factor authentication, proper email etiquette for academic settings, and identifying secure websites via HTTPS. Additionally, I incorporated two short-answer questions that require respondents to critically analyze a simulated phishing attempt and explain the long-term career impacts of their digital footprint.

Creating this form was an interesting exercise in assessment design. It challenged me to formulate clear, unambiguous questions and define grading rubrics for subjective short-answer responses, ensuring the quiz effectively measures practical digital survival skills for university students entering the modern global tech workforce today.
