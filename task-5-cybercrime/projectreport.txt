While researching cybercrime targeting college students, I was most surprised by the sheer psychological manipulation involved in modern scams. I previously thought cybercrime primarily involved sophisticated hackers writing code to breach secure databases. However, learning about social engineering tactics—like fake WhatsApp job offers or urgent "wrong transfer" UPI calls—showed me that scammers rely on human error, panic, and empathy rather than technical exploits. It is alarming how easily a fabricated sense of urgency can make an educated student bypass their own security common sense.

Because of this, the one habit I will personally change is my immediate reaction to unsolicited messages. Going forward, I will implement a strict "pause and verify" rule, refusing to click links or send money under pressure until I have independently verified the sender's identity through official channels.
