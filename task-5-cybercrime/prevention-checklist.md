Here are 8 actionable steps to protect your digital identity and finances:

1. **Lock Down Social Media:** Set your Instagram and Facebook profiles to private. Never share your live location, hostel room number, or daily schedule publicly.
2. **Beware of Job Scams:** Treat unsolicited WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering "easy part-time typing jobs" or "crypto investments" as immediate red flags.
3. **Use a Password Manager:** Stop reusing the same password for your university portal, email, and bank. Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords.
4. **Enable 2FA Everywhere:** Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for all essential accounts to add a layer of security beyond just your password.
5. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Logins:** Never log into your bank account or pay university fees while connected to free, unsecured Wi-Fi at a cafe or campus library.
6. **Update Your Devices:** Set your phone and laptop to update their operating systems automatically to patch new security vulnerabilities.
7. **UPI Safety Tip 1 (The Golden Rule):** You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If an app asks for your PIN, money is going to be deducted from your account.
8. **UPI Safety Tip 2 (Limit Exposure):** Go into your banking app and set a daily UPI transaction limit (e.g., ₹2,000) so that if your phone is compromised, the financial damage is capped.
